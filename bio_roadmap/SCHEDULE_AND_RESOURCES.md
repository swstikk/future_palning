   Best for: DNA, Central Dogma, Cell Biology, Genetics, Biochemistry

   Best modules: "DNA Replication", "Gene Regulation", "Epigenetics", "CRISPR"
   → Research-level insight, connects to cutting edge

🔴 MIT OpenCourseWare 7.012 (Introductory Biology)
   → Full MIT course FREE, Tier 1 & 2 level

🟠 NCERT 12th Biology (PDF) → Unit 2 (Genetics) + Unit 4 (Biotechnology)
   → In Hindi/English, good for foundations

🟠 Addgene CRISPR Guide → addgene.org/guides/crispr
   → Best CRISPR overview, FREE, written by practitioners
   → Tier 1B essential

🟠 PDB101 → pdb101.rcsb.org → Protein structure tutorials
   → Interactive 3D structures, great for Tier 2.1

🟡 Coursera: "Molecular Biology" (MIT) — structured video course
🟡 edX: "Cell Biology" (Harvard) — more advanced

═══════════════════════════════════════════
 BOOKS (in reading order)
═══════════════════════════════════════════
🔴 "Lifespan" — David Sinclair
   → READ FIRST! Inspires and gives aging overview
   → Easy to read, no heavy science prerequisite

🟠 "Molecular Biology of the Cell" — Alberts (7th Ed)
   → Reference book, DON'T read cover to cover!
   → Use targeted chapters: Ch4 (DNA), Ch6 (Gene Expression), Ch15 (Signaling), Ch17 (Cell Cycle)

🟡 "Molecular Biology of the Gene" — Watson
   → More mechanistic than Alberts, good for GE tier

🟡 "Aging" — Strehler → Classic aging biology textbook

═══════════════════════════════════════════
 KEY PAPERS (read in this order)
═══════════════════════════════════════════
1. Yamanaka 2006 → iPSC discovery (Nobel Prize, SHORT paper, read it!)
2. Horvath 2013 → DNA methylation clock (THE aging clock paper)
3. López-Otín et al. 2023 → "Hallmarks of Aging" (THE bible, updated version)
4. Baker et al. 2016 → Senescent cell clearance extends lifespan
5. Amor et al. 2024 → Senolytic CAR-T cells reverse aging
6. Jinek et al. 2012 → CRISPR-Cas9 as programmable endonuclease (Doudna's paper)
7. Ocampo et al. 2016 → Partial reprogramming extends lifespan
8. Lu et al. 2020 → OSK (no Myc) restores vision in aged mice
→ Find all papers: PubMed, bioRxiv, Semantic Scholar (all FREE)
