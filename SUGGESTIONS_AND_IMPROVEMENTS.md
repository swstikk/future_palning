* **ACTION ITEM:** When you study immunology and synthetic biology, focus specifically on CAR-T cell engineering. This is currently the most potent way to clear senescent cells, far outperforming drugs like Dasatinib + Quercetin.

## ⚠️ 3. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Skipping the 'Boring' Biology:** It is tempting to jump straight into Quantum Machine Learning and neural networks. However, AI is just a tool. The real value comes from knowing *which biological problems are worth solving*. A perfect ML model is useless if you don't understand the underlying biology of mTOR signaling or the limitations of RNA-seq data. Master Tier 1.
2. **The "Tutorial Hell" Trap:** Following step-by-step coding tutorials gives a false sense of competence. You must build projects from scratch.
    * *Instead of:* Watching a 3-hour tutorial on "How to build a neural network".
    * *Do this:* Read the documentation for PyTorch and try to build a simple classifier for a basic dataset (like the Iris dataset) without looking at a tutorial.
3. **Ignoring Data Wrangling in Bioinformatics:** The glamorous part of bioinformatics is the final analysis. The reality is that 80% of the job is cleaning messy data, converting file formats (FASTQ to BAM), and dealing with batch effects.
    * *Improvement:* Prioritize learning foundational RNA-Seq and scRNA-Seq pipelines early. Get comfortable in the Linux command line. It is arguably more important than knowing the exact math behind a support vector machine right now.
4. **Over-Indexing on Pure Quantum Computing:** True fault-tolerant quantum computers that can simulate complex molecules are still years away.
    * *Improvement:* Focus on **Hybrid Classical-Quantum algorithms (like VQE)**. This is what you have planned in the Prometheus blueprint, which is excellent. Stick to that plan. Learn PyTorch and JAX deeply, as these frameworks are often used in hybrid pipelines (like PennyLane).
