* **The Breakthrough:** MIT recently released Boltz-1, an open-source deep learning model achieving AlphaFold3-level accuracy in predicting 3D structures of biomolecular complexes. It's fully open-source (MIT license).
* **Why it matters to you:** You no longer need a massive wet lab to do advanced protein design or test senolytic drug targets. You can simulate protein-protein interactions (crucial for aging research) purely computationally.

* **The Breakthrough:** Late 2024/Early 2025 research confirms SGLT2 inhibitors (traditionally diabetes drugs) clear senescent cells, *not* by direct apoptosis, but by enhancing endogenous immunosurveillance (immune system clearance).
* **Why it matters to you:** This shifts the paradigm of "Longevity" from just finding toxic chemicals that kill old cells to metabolic reprogramming and immune system activation.
* **Actionable Update:** In your Bio Roadmap (Tier 3.1: Hallmarks of Aging & 3.2 Immunology), prioritize the link between metabolism (AMPK pathways, though focus heavily on immunosurveillance) and cellular senescence.

### C. CRISPR Casgevy & LNP Delivery Expansion

* **The Breakthrough:** CRISPR Therapeutics reported robust launches for CASGEVY (first approved CRISPR therapy for Sickle Cell/Beta Thalassemia). Crucially, they are heavily expanding into *in vivo* therapies using Lipid Nanoparticle (LNP) delivery to target the liver for complex diseases.
* **Why it matters to you:** "Genetic Engineering" isn't just about Cas9 cutting DNA anymore; the bottleneck is *delivery*. LNPs are the future of getting edits into living human tissue.
* **Actionable Update:** When you study Recombinant DNA (GE.3) and Gene Delivery (GE.2), spend 70% of your time on LNP design, viral vectors (AAVs), and tissue-specific targeting, rather than just the Cas9 protein mechanism.

### D. Quantum Computing Meets AI (Quantinuum Helios)
