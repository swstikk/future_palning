## ⚠️ 3. COMMON PITFALLS & MISTAKES TO AVOID

As a highly ambitious 16-year-old, these are the traps you must actively avoid:

### ❌ Mistake 1: "Tutorial Hell"

* **The Trap**: Watching hundreds of hours of Khan Academy or MIT OCW without writing a single line of code or solving a problem set yourself. You feel like you are learning, but you are just passively consuming.
* **The Fix**: For every 1 hour of video, you must spend 1 hour implementing or explaining the concept. If you learn matrix multiplication, write a Python script to do it from scratch without NumPy.

### ❌ Mistake 2: Skipping the Math

* **The Trap**: Jumping straight to Qiskit or PyTorch because it's fun to see the output, while ignoring Linear Algebra and Calculus.
* **The Reality**: You cannot read a single advanced quantum biology or ML paper without understanding tensors, eigenvalues, and gradients.
* **The Fix**: Honor the *Project Prometheus* blueprint. Modules M1-M4 are non-negotiable.

### ❌ Mistake 3: Treating Biology as "Just Memorization"

* **The Trap**: Trying to memorize protein names like historical dates.
* **The Reality**: Biology is a highly complex, interconnected systems-engineering problem.
* **The Fix**: Think of the cell as a computer. DNA is the hard drive, RNA is the RAM, Ribosomes are the compiler, and Proteins are the executables. When a mutation happens, it's a bug in the code. Approach genetics from a systems-level perspective.

### ❌ Mistake 4: Premature Optimization in Algo Trading

* **The Trap**: Spending weeks tweaking parameters on a trading algorithm to squeeze out 0.1% more profit on historical data (overfitting).
* **The Fix**: Use Algo Trading strictly as a sandbox to practice Data Science, time-series analysis, and API integration. If it makes money, great. But its primary purpose is to make you a better data scientist for biology.
