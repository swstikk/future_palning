### 2. METHODOLOGY IMPROVEMENTS (The "3-4 Hour Constraint")

Given you are a 16-year-old student balancing Class 11/12 with a highly ambitious roadmap (Bio, QC, ML, Algo Trading), avoiding burnout is your primary directive.

#### 🚫 Avoid "Tutorial Hell"

* **Problem:** Watching endless Khan Academy or MIT OCW videos without writing code or solving problems.
* **Fix:** **Implementation-First Learning**. For every 1 hour of theory, you must spend 1 hour building. E.g., if you learn about DNA translation, immediately write a Python script to translate a nucleotide sequence.

#### 📈 Algorithmic Trading as an ML Sandbox

* **Suggestion:** Since you want to learn Algo Trading and Machine Learning simultaneously, **combine them**.
* **Execution:** Do not learn ML on boring "Titanic survival" datasets. Learn Time-Series Forecasting, Reinforcement Learning (RL), and Sequence Models by predicting market data. Financial data is noisy, vast, and free—it is the ultimate training ground for deep learning before you move to complex biological data (like RNA-seq).

#### 🧬 Start with Single-Cell RNA Sequencing (scRNA-seq)

* **Suggestion:** In your bioinformatics journey, prioritize scRNA-seq analysis early. Aging is a heterogeneous process (different cells age differently). The longevity field has moved away from bulk RNA-seq to single-cell resolution.
* **Execution:** Learn tools like `Scanpy` (Python) or `Seurat` (R) as soon as you finish basic Python programming.

#### ⚛️ The PyTorch / JAX Imperative

* **Suggestion:** While learning Qiskit is good, the entire AI-Bio industry (including the teams that built Boltz-1 and AlphaFold) uses **PyTorch** and **JAX** for differentiable programming.
* **Execution:** Make PyTorch/JAX your primary coding languages for ML and Bio-simulation.
