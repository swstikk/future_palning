## ⚙️ 2. ROADMAP IMPROVEMENTS & BOTTLENECK FIXES

### The "3-4 Hour Constraint" Reality Check

You cannot do Bio + ML + Quantum + Algo Trading simultaneously every single day in 4 hours. If you try, you will make micro-progress in all and master none.

**Improvement 1: Implement "Serial-Parallel" Focus (Sprints)**
Instead of doing 4 things a day, structure your year into Sprints.

* **Quarter 1 (Jan-Mar):** Focus 80% on ML/Python/Algo Trading. (Algo trading gives you immediate, fast feedback loops to test your Python and statistics skills).
* **Quarter 2 (Apr-Jun):** Focus 80% on Biology & Bioinformatics. Apply the Python/ML skills from Q1 to biological datasets (e.g., RNA-seq, Boltz-1).
* **Quarter 3 (Jul-Sep):** Focus 80% on Math & Quantum Computing.
* *Note: Dedicate the remaining 20% of your time to maintaining spaced repetition (Anki) for the other subjects so you don't forget them.*

**Improvement 2: Merge Your Goals (Synergy)**
Stop treating these subjects as separate.

* *Don't learn ML on toy datasets (like predicting house prices).* Learn ML by building models that predict protein binding affinities or classify DNA sequences.
* *Don't learn Algo Trading in a vacuum.* Use it to master Time-Series Analysis and Deep Reinforcement Learning. The exact same RL math used to maximize trading profits is used to optimize molecular drug design pathways.

**Improvement 3: Avoid "Tutorial Hell"**
Your syllabus is very heavily dependent on Khan Academy, MIT OCW, and YouTube.

* *The Trap:* Watching a 3Blue1Brown video feels like learning, but it's passive.
* *The Fix:* For every 1 hour of video, you must write code for 1 hour. If you learn Matrix Multiplication, code it from scratch in NumPy. If you learn the Central Dogma, write a Python script to transcribe DNA to mRNA.
